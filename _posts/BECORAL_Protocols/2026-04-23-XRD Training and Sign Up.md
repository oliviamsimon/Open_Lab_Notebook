# XRD Training and Sign Up

*X-ray diffraction is a non-distructive technique to determine the mineralogy of a sample. By definition ([of Bragg’s Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bragg%27s_law)), it works on crystalline samples. Hence, it is minimally informative if you’re interested in amorphous phases. Relative phase abundance of multi-phase crystalline samples can be determined using the [Relative Intensity Ratio](https://journals.iucr.org/paper?s0021889874010375) against consistent reference patterns.*

## TRAINING

1. Determine whether you want to analyze bulk powder samples (most likely) or single crystal/very small samples. Bulk powder samples are run on the Phillips X’Pert Powder X-Ray Diffractometer.

2. At a minimum, you’ll need 10 mg of sample. Aim for 100 mg of sample. It can be cleaned or not; there is lower background interference from cleaned samples, but some cleaning solutions can cause recrystallization. It can also be powdered or intact mineral; powdered samples will give a cleaner signal, also with lower background.

3. Email Tom Emge, with Fio in cc, that you’d like to be trained to use an XRD. Specify which instrument. Together you will choose an in-person training time on the instrument.

4. Email Leo Trooskin at REHS that you’re being trained by Tom Emge on the XRD and that you need to request a ring dosimeter.

5. Create a QReserve account [here](https://my.qreserve.com/login). After validating your email, type your first and last name into the Name bar (both first and last name are required). Then enter Rutgers under the Institution search option. Then choose Rutgers Chemistry Department as the membership to join. From there, choose the X-Ray dropdown on the right. We will likely be getting trained on the Phillips X’Pert Powder Diffractometer. Click on the date during which you want to reserve your instrument time and complete the rest of the reservation form (no account number is required to make the reservation). The powder diffractometer usage cost is $20/hr.

6. Take the X-Ray Training training in the [myREHS portal](https://myrehs.rutgers.edu/). From the Main Menu page, click on the Training Calendar button and then scroll down until you see online training links at the bottom of the screen. Choose the X-Ray Training link and follow the rest of the prompts. The training is one page long and should take less than 15 minutes to complete.

7. An email will be generated upon successful completion of the X-ray training with a link to a completion certification. Save the certificate pdf and email a copy to Tom.

11. Your in-person XRD training with Tom will be held in the XRD lab. If possible, take some samples with you so that you can familiarize yourself with handling your samples relative to the instrument and processing the data.
